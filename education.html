Care Guides

These short guides give clear, practical tips for caring for dogs in Samoa's warm, tropical climate.

Flea & Tick Prevention

Samoa‚Äôs humidity makes fleas and ticks common. Check your dog‚Äôs skin weekly, especially around ears, tail, and belly. Use monthly flea/tick prevention and keep sleeping areas clean and dry.

Basic Nutrition

Island dogs stay healthiest with balanced meals. Combine quality kibble with cooked local foods (pumpkin, taro, chicken, fish). Avoid toxic foods such as chocolate, grapes, onions, and anything salty or seasoned.

Heat Safety

Daytime heat can be dangerous. Provide shade, cool water, and rest during midday. Walk dogs early morning or late afternoon. Watch for signs of heat stress: heavy drooling, bright red tongue, wobbling, or vomiting.

Spaying/neutering prevents unwanted litters and reduces roaming and aggression. It helps control Samoa‚Äôs stray population and improves long-term health by lowering risks of infections and cancers.
