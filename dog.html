How Adoption Works

Every dog in Samoa deserves a safe place to sleep, enough food, and people who care. This page explains how the adoption process typically works with local rescues and clinics.

1. Find a Dog

Start on the Adopt page to browse dogs currently available. Many come from local vets, foster homes, or volunteers who found them on the streets.

2. Contact the Caretaker

Each dog has someone responsible for their care. This might be a clinic, a foster family, or a small rescue group. They can tell you about the dog‚Äôs temperament, health history, and what kind of home would fit best.

3. Prepare Your Home

Before bringing a dog home, make a safe sleeping area, plan for regular feeding, and review the Education page so you know how to handle pests, heat, and basic training.

4. Follow Up Care

Many dogs will need vaccines, deworming, and possibly spay/neuter surgery. Staying in touch with your vet and local rescues helps keep your new family member healthy and happy.
